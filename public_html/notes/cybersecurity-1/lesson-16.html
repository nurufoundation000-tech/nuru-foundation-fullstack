WEEK 6 — CLASS 1

MALWARE & ATTACKS

Understanding the Enemy

☠️ The Digital Battleground
To defend a system, you must first understand the weapons used to destroy it.

⭐ 1. Today’s Mission

Today you will study the primary enemy of cybersecurity — MALWARE.

Malware is responsible for:

  • Data Theft: Stealing identities and bank details.
  • System Destruction: Making computers unusable.
  • Financial Loss: Extorting money from victims.
  • Surveillance: Watching you through your own camera.

By the end of this class, you will learn to think like a Cyber Threat Analyst.

☠️ 2. What Is Malware?

Malware = Malicious Software. It is any program designed to damage, steal, or gain unauthorized access.

Who creates it?

  • Hackers: Looking for a challenge or "clout."
  • Cybercriminals: Motivated purely by money.
  • Organized Groups: Working for governments or large cartels to perform espionage.

🧨 3. Types of Malware (The Core Three)

  • 🦠 1. Virus: Attaches to a "host" file. It cannot spread unless a human opens that file. (Needs User Action)
  • 🐛 2. Worm: A standalone program that self-replicates. It crawls through network cables and Wi-Fi to infect every PC in a building automatically.
  • 🐴 3. Trojan Horse: Disguises itself as something useful (like a free game or a PDF converter). Once opened, it creates a "Backdoor."
Real Threat: A RAT (Remote Access Trojan) allows a hacker to move your mouse and see your screen from across the world!

🔐 4. Ransomware & Spyware

These two are currently the most profitable tools for cybercriminals.

  • Ransomware: Encrypts (locks) all your photos and documents. It demands a payment (Bitcoin) to get them back.
  • Spyware: Secretly records your activity. A Keylogger is a type of spyware that records every single key you press to steal your passwords.

🤖 5. Adware & Rootkits

  • ⛏️ Adware: Floods your system with unwanted ads. While less "dangerous," it tracks your data and slows down your PC significantly.
  • 👻 Rootkits: The most dangerous! These hide deep inside the system Kernel. They can hide from your Antivirus, making them almost impossible to find without special tools.

Logic Bomb: A hidden piece of code set to "explode" (delete data) on a specific date or when a specific action happens.

🧠 4. How Malware Spreads

Hackers use "Delivery Vehicles" to get malware onto your device:

  • 📧 Phishing Emails: Tricking you into clicking a bad link.
  • 🌐 Malicious Websites: "Drive-by downloads" that infect you just by visiting the page.
  • 💾 USB Devices: Plugging in an unknown flash drive can infect a PC in seconds.
  • 📲 Fake Apps: Downloading apps from unofficial stores that look like real games.

🔍 5. Detecting Malware

Warning Signs: 1. Computer is very slow. 2. Strange pop-ups. 3. Antivirus turns itself off. 4. Files won't open.

Professional Detection Methods:

  • Signature-Based: Antivirus looks for "fingerprints" of known malware.
  • Heuristics (Behavioral): Monitoring what a program does. If a "Calculator" app tries to delete your system files, it is blocked!
  • Sandboxing: Running a suspicious file in a "safe box" to see if it acts maliciously.

🧠 6. Analyst Thinking

A Security Analyst doesn't just react; they predict. Ask yourself:

  • ❓ "Why is this file 50MB when it's just a text document?"
  • ❓ "Why is my internet uploading a lot of data at 3:00 AM?"
  • ❓ "Where did this 'Free Software' actually come from?"

Suspicion is your greatest defense.

🎯 End of Class 1 Outcome

  • ✔ Identify the dangers of Ransomware and Keyloggers.
  • ✔ Understand how Rootkits hide from defenses.
  • ✔ Recognize the delivery methods used by attackers.
