BECOMING A NETWORK DETECTIVE

Week 2 — Class 2 (Practical Session)

⭐ Today's Mission: You will discover your own digital footprints, trace connections across the globe, and map your digital neighborhood. This isn't theory—it's investigation.

1. Discovering Your Digital Identity

Every device on a network leaves traces. These three pieces of information form your Network Fingerprint:

  • IPv4 Address: Your specific location in the network.
  • MAC Address (Physical Address): Your device's permanent, unique ID.
  • Default Gateway: The address of your router (your door to the internet).
C:\Users\Maca> ipconfig

Step: Open CMD (Windows) or Terminal (Mac/Linux) and find these three values.

2. Network Tools: The Echo Test (ping)

Ping checks if a device is reachable. It sends a small packet and waits for a reply.

C:\Users\Maca> ping google.com
C:\Users\Maca> ping 8.8.8.8

✅ Reply Received

The device is alive, online, and reachable.

❌ Request Timed Out

The device is offline or has a firewall blocking you.

3. Tracing the Path (tracert)

Tracert (Windows) or Traceroute (Mac) shows every "hop" your data takes to reach a destination.

C:\Users\Maca> tracert google.com

Insight: Your data doesn't go straight to Google. It travels through your router, your ISP, sub-sea cables, and multiple cities before it arrives!

4. NSLookup: The Phonebook

Every website has an IP address. NSLookup helps you find it.

C:\Users\Maca> nslookup facebook.com

Security Insight: Before launching an attack, hackers use this tool to gather intelligence. They need to know the IP address of the server they are targeting.

5. Creating Your First Network Map

A Network Map is a blueprint. It helps you see every device that could be a "weak link" in your security.

[Internet] -> [Router 192.168.1.1]
    ├── Laptop (Strong Password ✓)
    ├── Phone (Regular Updates ✓)
    └── Smart TV (Default Password ❌ RISK!)

Identify Risks: Look for devices with default passwords, old software (firmware), or devices you don't recognize.

6. Seeing Through the Hacker's Eyes

Before a hacker attacks, they do Reconnaissance (Information Gathering).

  • Public IP: Tells them your ISP and your general city/location.
  • Trace Path: Tells them which network companies are handling your data.
  • Device List: Tells them which device is the easiest to break into.

Exercise: Visit whatismyipaddress.com and see how much information is visible to the world right now.

✅ Network Detective Checklist

Ensure you can confidently do the following:

  • I know my Private IP and MAC.
  • I can identify my Gateway.
  • I can test connections (Ping).
  • I can find Website IPs (NSLookup).
  • I can trace data hops (Tracert).
  • I understand my Public IP info.
  • I have mapped my home devices.
  • I can spot a security risk on a map.
