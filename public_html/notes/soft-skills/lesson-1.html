Communication Skills

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the importance of effective communication
  • Learn verbal and non-verbal communication techniques
  • Develop active listening skills
  • Practice clear and concise message delivery

Why Communication Skills Matter

Effective communication is the foundation of success in work and life. Whether you're speaking with friends, presenting ideas at work, or building relationships, how you communicate determines your impact.

Key Benefits of Good Communication

  • Builds Stronger Relationships: Clear communication fosters trust and understanding
  • Increases Career Success: Good communicators are more likely to be promoted
  • Reduces Conflicts: Misunderstandings are minimized when messages are clear
  • Boosts Confidence: Expressing yourself clearly makes you feel more confident
Did You Know?

Studies show that up to 93% of communication effectiveness is determined by non-verbal cues, while only 7% comes from the actual words spoken!

Verbal Communication Skills

Verbal communication involves the words you choose and how you deliver them.

Practical Tips for Better Verbal Communication

  • Be Clear and Concise: Get to the point without unnecessary details
  • Use Simple Language: Avoid jargon unless your audience understands it
  • Match Your Tone: Adjust your energy level to suit the situation
  • Ask Clarifying Questions: Ensure understanding by asking "What do you mean?"
  • Pause Before Responding: Take a moment to think before speaking
Pro Tip

When explaining something complex, use the " sandwich method": start with a simple overview, give details, then summarize the key point.

Non-Verbal Communication

Your body language, facial expressions, and tone of voice speak volumes even before you say a word.

Essential Non-Verbal Elements

  • Eye Contact: Maintain appropriate eye contact to show engagement (not too intense, not looking away)
  • Posture: Stand or sit upright to appear confident and interested
  • Gestures: Use hand movements naturally to emphasize points
  • Facial Expressions: Match your expressions to your message - smile when appropriate
  • Physical Space: Respect personal boundaries - don't stand too close or too far
Watch Out

Crossed arms, looking at your phone, or fidgeting can signal disinterest even if your words say otherwise. Be aware of your non-verbal cues!

Active Listening

Great communicators are also great listeners. Active listening means fully concentrating on what is being said rather than just passively hearing the message.

Active Listening Techniques

  • Give Full Attention: Put away distractions and focus on the speaker
  • Show You're Listening: Nod, make small comments like "I see", "Go on"
  • Don't Interrupt: Let the speaker finish before responding
  • Paraphrase: Repeat back what you heard in your own words
  • Ask Questions: Clarify anything you don't understand
Practice This

Next time someone speaks to you, try to summarize their main point before responding. Say: "So what I'm hearing is..."

Practice Exercise

Communication Self-Assessment

  1. Think of a recent conversation where you felt misunderstood. What went wrong?
  2. Identify one verbal and one non-verbal cue you could improve
  3. Practice with a friend: Share a story for 2 minutes while they practice active listening
  4. Switch roles and discuss how it felt to be actively listened to
