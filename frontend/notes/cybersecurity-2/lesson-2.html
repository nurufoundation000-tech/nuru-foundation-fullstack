🛠️ Introducing Wireshark

Wireshark is the world's most popular network protocol analyzer. It captures packets from your network interfaces and lets you inspect them in detail.

What Wireshark Does
  • Captures live network traffic from any interface (WiFi, Ethernet, etc.)
  • Displays packets with full details (headers, payload, timestamps)
  • Decodes 2000+ protocols (TCP, HTTP, DNS, ARP, etc.)
  • Filters traffic to focus on specific conversations
  • Saves captures for later analysis
Why Cybersecurity Pros Use It
  • Debug network issues – Why is the connection slow?
  • Investigate attacks – What did the malware send?
  • Baseline normal traffic – What does "healthy" look like?
  • Forensic analysis – What happened during the breach?

Key mindset: Wireshark shows you what is actually happening on the wire, not what you think is happening.

🔧 Hands-On Lab: Capturing Traffic

Follow these exact steps on your machine.

1

Download & Install Wireshark
Get it from wireshark.org (Windows/Mac/Linux).

2

Launch Wireshark
wireshark (Linux/Mac terminal) or double-click the icon (Windows).

3

Select Network Interface
Look for your active interface:
eth0 (Ethernet) | wlan0/Wi-Fi (Wireless)

📸 Expected: You should see a list of interfaces with packet rates. Choose the one with traffic!

📡 Complete Capture Process

4

Start Capture
Click the blue shark fin 🦈 button or press Ctrl+E.

5

Generate Traffic
Open a new terminal and run:
ping google.com

6

Stop Capture
Click the red square ⏹️ or Ctrl+E again after 10-15 seconds.

What Just Happened?

Your ping command created ICMP packets that traveled to Google's servers and back. Wireshark captured every single one!

🔍 What You See in Wireshark

Your capture now shows three columns of information about every packet:

Source IP
192.168.1.100 (your laptop)
Destination IP
142.250.190.14 (Google DNS)
Protocol
ICMP
Packet Size
84 bytes

First discovery: Even a simple ping generates dozens of packets with precise details about timing, size, and routing!

🧬 Drilling Into Packet Details

Click any packet row to see the magic. Wireshark shows three panes:

Packet List (Top Pane)
  • Summary of all captured packets
  • Color-coded by protocol
  • Shows packet number, time, source, destination
Packet Details (Middle Pane)
  • Folded tree view of headers
  • Click to expand Ethernet → IP → ICMP layers
  • Protocol-specific information decoded
Packet Bytes (Bottom Pane)

Raw hexadecimal and ASCII dump of the packet. This is what travels on the wire!

📸 Pro tip: Right-click → "Follow → TCP Stream" to see complete conversations!

🎛️ Essential Wireshark Filters

Filters let you focus on specific traffic. Type in the filter bar and press Enter:

Traffic Filters
  • ip.src == 192.168.1.100 – Your machine only
  • ip.dst == 8.8.8.8 – Traffic to Google DNS
  • tcp.port == 80 – HTTP web traffic
  • dns – All DNS queries
Attack Investigation Filters
  • arp – ARP spoofing detection
  • bootp – DHCP traffic
  • icmp – Ping sweeps/scans
  • http contains "password" – Cleartext credentials

💡 Filter power: Turn millions of packets into 10 relevant ones instantly!
