PRACTICAL: WINDOWS DEFENDER & FIREWALL

System Shielding: Today you step into the role of a System Administrator to secure a real Windows environment.

⭐ 1. Today’s Mission

You will secure your workstation using the built-in Windows security suite. You will:

  • 🛡️ Configure Antivirus: Set up real-time defense.
  • 🔥 Enable Firewalls: Lock the digital doors to your network.
  • 🔍 Scan for Threats: Hunt for hidden malware.

🛠️ 2. Lab 1 — Windows Defender Setup

Windows Defender is your built-in police force. Let's make sure they are on duty.

Steps to Open:

  1. Click the Start Menu (Windows Icon).
  2. Search for Windows Security and press Enter.
  3. Look for the "Security at a glance" home screen.
Goal: All icons should have a Green Checkmark ✅. If you see a yellow triangle ⚠️, a shield is down!

🔐 Check Your Protection Pillars

Inside Windows Security, ensure these three areas are active:

  • 🦠 Virus & threat protection: Monitors files for malicious code.
  • 🔥 Firewall & network protection: Blocks hackers from the web.
  • 🌐 App & browser control: Protects you while you surf the internet.

Pro-Tip: If you have a 3rd-party antivirus (like Norton or McAfee), it might take over these settings. In a pro environment, Windows Defender is often all you need!

🟢 Run a Quick Scan

Let's check if any "burglars" are currently hiding in your system files.

  1. Click on Virus & threat protection.
  2. Click the Quick Scan button.
  3. Wait for the results. (It checks folders where malware usually hides).
Difference: A Quick Scan takes 2 mins. A Full Scan checks every single file on your computer and can take hours!

🔥 3. Lab 2 — Firewall Configuration

The Firewall is your "Digital Sieve." It keeps the trash out while letting the water (data) through.

Steps:

  1. In Windows Security, click Firewall & network protection.
  2. Ensure all three network types are ON.

🧱 Which Gate are you Guarding?

Windows treats different Wi-Fi connections differently. You must secure all of them:

  • 🏠 Private Network: Used for home or office. Devices can "talk" to each other (like your PC talking to your Printer).
  • ☕ Public Network: Used for Starbucks or Airports. Most Dangerous! This setting hides your PC from others on the same Wi-Fi.
  • 🏢 Domain Network: Used in large companies managed by a central server.

🚨 If the Firewall is OFF, anyone on your Wi-Fi can try to "ping" or enter your computer!

🧠 4. Lab Discussion

Think about what you just did and answer these questions:

  • ❓ Antivirus Off: If we turn it off to install a "free game," why is that a huge risk?
  • ❓ Public Wi-Fi: Why should you NEVER turn off your firewall while at a coffee shop?
  • ❓ Firewall's Job: Does a firewall stop a virus that you downloaded yourself? (Hint: No, that's the Antivirus's job!)
