Introduction to Cybersecurity

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what cybersecurity is and why it matters
  • Identify common types of cyber threats
  • Recognize the importance of cybersecurity in daily life
  • Learn basic cybersecurity concepts and terminology

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, programs, and data from unauthorized access or digital attacks. It involves a set of technologies, processes, and practices designed to safeguard systems from malicious activities.

Why Cybersecurity Matters

  • Protection of Personal Data: Safeguards your private information from theft
  • Financial Security: Prevents fraud and monetary losses
  • Privacy Protection: Keeps your digital life secure
  • Business Continuity: Ensures organizations can operate safely
Did You Know?

Cyber attacks occur every 39 seconds, affecting 1 in 3 Americans every year!

Common Cyber Threats

Understanding the types of threats is the first step in protecting yourself.

Types of Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software including viruses, worms, and trojans
  • Phishing: Deceptive emails or websites designed to steal information
  • Ransomware: Encrypts files and demands payment for recovery
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating people into revealing confidential information
  • Man-in-the-Middle Attacks: Intercepting communications between two parties
  • Denial of Service (DoS): Overloading systems to make them unavailable
Important

Cyber criminals often target the weakest link - human users. Being aware is your first line of defense!

Basic Security Concepts

These fundamental concepts form the foundation of cybersecurity practices.

The CIA Triad

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring information is accessible only to authorized individuals
  • Integrity: Maintaining accuracy and completeness of data
  • Availability: Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed

Defense in Depth

Using multiple layers of security controls throughout a system. If one layer fails, others still provide protection.

Pro Tip

Think of cybersecurity like onion layers - the more layers, the harder it is for attackers to reach your data!

Your Role in Cybersecurity

Every user plays a crucial role in maintaining security. Simple habits can make a big difference.

Best Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Keep software and systems updated
  • Be cautious with email attachments and links
  • Use secure Wi-Fi connections
  • Back up important data regularly
  • Report suspicious activities immediately
