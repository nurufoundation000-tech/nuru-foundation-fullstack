🔑 LAB 2 — OBSERVING BRUTE-FORCE ATTEMPTS

A brute-force attack is when an attacker tries many passwords very fast, hoping to guess the right one.

Millions of automated brute-force attacks are happening right now on:

  • Gmail, Facebook, WordPress sites.
  • Servers, Wi-Fi routers, Databases, and Admin dashboards.

What You Will Observe:

Go to any threat map and look specifically for terms like:

SSH brute-force login attempts | RDP login attempts | Web login brute-force

What You Learn from Lab 2

You will notice that these automated attacks never stop, come from many countries, and target random servers constantly.

✔ Key Takeaways:

  • Why weak passwords are destroyed in seconds.
  • Why every online service needs 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication).
  • Why bots and automation make hacking extremely fast and scalable.
  • Why companies use firewalls to block this constant traffic.

After doing this lab, you will NEVER use a weak password again.

🕵️‍♂️ LAB 3 — IDENTIFYING REAL PHISHING PAGES

Phishing is the #1 attack vector used to steal passwords, OTPs, money, and bank accounts.

⭐ What You Will Do: Search online for “real phishing website examples” and compare 2–3 sample pages with the real, legitimate websites.

✔ Things to Look For:

1. Strange URL

Examples: facebo0k-security-help.com or paypall-update.net. If the domain looks unfamiliar or has typos, it is fake.

2. Urgent Messages

Phishing always creates fear: "Your account will be deleted in 1 hour!" or "Payment failed — update immediately!" Fear is their weapon.

More Phishing Warning Signs

  • Fake Login Form: Asks for email + password + OTP at once, or has "sign up" disabled.
  • Visual Mistakes: Blurry logo, missing icons, poor grammar, or strange spacing/colors.
  • Suspicious Behavior: Login button redirects too quickly, or the page loads too fast.
  • Asks Too Much: The page requires unnecessary personal or financial information upfront.

🧼 LAB 4 — CREATE YOUR DAILY CYBER HYGIENE ROUTINE

Cybersecurity is not just tools—it is habits. Your goal: Build a daily routine that protects you every single day. A simple checklist that you follow without thinking.

⭐ What Your Cyber Hygiene Should Include:

Morning/Start of Day

  • Check email for suspicious messages.
  • Avoid clicking any unknown link.
  • Avoid open/public Wi-Fi; use VPN.
  • Check that devices updated overnight.

Throughout the Day

  • Never save passwords in browsers.
  • Never download files you do not trust.
  • Confirm any URL before entering a password.
  • Avoid free random apps or APK files.
  • Think twice before sharing personal info.

Evening/End of Day

  • Backup important files.
  • Log out from sensitive accounts.
  • Disconnect unused devices.
  • Clear browser cookies.
  • Check for email login alerts.

Weekly Behavior

  • Update all apps and OS.
  • Change passwords for risky accounts.
  • Review bank/SIM/email activity.
  • Scan your device with antivirus.
  • Delete old, unused apps.
