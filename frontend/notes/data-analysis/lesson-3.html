Data Presentation

Learning Objectives

  • Create effective data dashboards
  • Write clear analytical reports
  • Build compelling data visualizations
  • Present findings to stakeholders effectively

Creating Effective Dashboards

A dashboard is a visual display of key metrics and KPIs that provides at-a-glance information for monitoring performance and making decisions.

Dashboard Components

  • KPI Cards: High-level metrics with trend indicators
  • Charts: Visual representations of data patterns
  • Tables: Detailed data with sorting and filtering
  • Filters: Interactive elements to drill down into data

Dashboard Design Principles

  1. Start with user needs: What decisions will this dashboard support?
  2. Prioritize information: Most important metrics at the top
  3. Use consistent design: Same colors, fonts, and layouts
  4. Limit visual complexity: Avoid overwhelming the user
  5. Add context: Include benchmarks, targets, and comparisons
Best Practice: A good dashboard should answer questions in under 5 seconds. Place the most critical information in the top-left corner (where users start scanning).

Writing Analytical Reports

Analytical reports translate data insights into actionable recommendations. A well-structured report guides the reader from understanding the problem to accepting your recommendations.

Report Structure

  1. Executive Summary: Key findings and recommendations (1 page)
  2. Introduction: Background and objectives
  3. Methodology: How the analysis was performed
  4. Findings: Detailed results with supporting data
  5. Recommendations: Actionable next steps
  6. Appendix: Technical details and data sources

Writing Tips

  • Lead with the most important insight
  • Use clear, simple language—avoid jargon
  • Support claims with data and visualizations
  • Connect findings to business impact
  • Make recommendations specific and actionable
Tip: Before writing, ask yourself: "What is the one thing I want the reader to remember after reading this report?" Structure everything around that key message.

Data Visualization Best Practices

Effective visualizations communicate insights clearly and accurately.

Line Charts

Show trends over time

Bar Charts

Compare categories

Pie Charts

Show proportions (max 5 slices)

Scatter Plots

Show relationships

Chart Selection Guide

  • Comparison: Bar charts, column charts
  • Trend over time: Line charts, area charts
  • Part-to-whole: Pie charts, treemaps
  • Distribution: Histograms, box plots
  • Relationship: Scatter plots, bubble charts

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • 3D charts that distort proportions
  • Truncated axes that mislead
  • Too many colors and chart types
  • Missing labels and legends
  • Charts without titles or context

Presenting to Stakeholders

Effective presentation skills help you communicate your findings and drive action.

Presentation Tips

  • Know your audience: Tailor technical depth to their expertise
  • Tell a story: Structure findings as a narrative
  • Focus on impact: Connect data to business outcomes
  • Anticipate questions: Prepare for objections
  • Keep it interactive: Engage stakeholders in discussion

Handling Q&A

  • Listen fully before responding
  • Clarify questions you don't understand
  • It's okay to say "I'll look into that"
  • Use data to support your answers

Summary

  • Dashboards provide at-a-glance monitoring of key metrics
  • Analytical reports should be structured with clear recommendations
  • Choose the right visualization type for your data and message
  • Tailor presentations to your audience and focus on business impact
